Russia-Ukraine War

Understanding the ongoing conflict

Introduction

The Russia-Ukraine war, which began in 2014 and escalated in 2022, has significantly impacted global geopolitics. This page provides an overview of the conflict's key aspects and consequences.

Timeline of Key Events

  • 2014: Annexation of Crimea by Russia.
  • 2015: Minsk II agreement signed but not fully implemented.
  • 2022: Russia launches a full-scale invasion of Ukraine.
  • 2023: Continued fighting and global efforts for peace.

Impact of the Conflict

  • Humanitarian Crisis

    Millions of people displaced, with significant loss of life and infrastructure.

  • Economic Consequences

    Rising energy prices and disruption of global food supplies.

  • Political Repercussions

    Increased tensions between NATO and Russia.

Conclusion

The Russia-Ukraine war continues to shape international relations, highlighting the importance of diplomacy and humanitarian aid in resolving conflicts.
